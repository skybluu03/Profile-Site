Summary

This project focused on assembling, annotating, and analyzing a microbial genome using Bash tools and Galaxy.eu. Our team performed quality control, optimized read trimming, and compared multiple assembly methods before selecting the best assembly based on QUAST metrics. Protein annotation and analysis were then carried out using PROKKA, RAST, sequence alignment, phylogenetic analysis, and structural prediction tools. Finally, the target protein, D-inositol-3-phosphate glycosyltransferase, was visualized in 3D and inserted into the pET-28a(+) plasmid for potential downstream applications.
